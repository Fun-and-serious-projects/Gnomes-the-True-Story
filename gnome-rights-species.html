Biological Classification

Scientific Name: Homo Horticulturalis Miniaturis

Despite popular misconception, we are NOT a subset of fairies, magical creatures, or lawn decorations. We are a proud, distinct species with rich cultural heritage.

Physical Characteristics

  • Average Height: 12-18 inches (We prefer "vertically challenged" to "short")
  • Distinctive Features: Pointy hats, robust beards, impeccable fashion sense
  • Skin Tone: Varies from earthy terracotta to pale garden-shade
  • Life Expectancy: 250-300 years (Significantly longer than human garden renovation cycles)

Cultural Practices

Contrary to human belief, we do not spend our days frozen in picturesque poses. Our society is complex, with intricate social structures, a rich oral history, and a deep commitment to garden ecosystem management.

  • Primary Occupations: Soil Guardians, Ecosystem Consultants, Underground Network Administrators
  • Social Hierarchy: Determined by hat height and mushroom cultivation skills
  • Communication: Primarily through underground root networks and occasional garden gnome sign language

Habitat and Survival

We are master adapters, thriving in diverse environments from meticulously maintained suburban gardens to wild, untamed wilderness. Our survival skills include:

  • Camouflage techniques that put military special forces to shame
  • Ability to remain motionless for decades when human surveillance is high
  • Advanced photosynthesis-adjacent metabolic processes
