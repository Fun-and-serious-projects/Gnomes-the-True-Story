1803: The Great Gnome Awakening

Our founder, Sir Gnomington Tallworthy, first proposed that gnomes were more than mere garden decorations. His groundbreaking manifesto, "We Are Not Lawn Ornaments," sparked the gnome rights movement.

1921: The Shrubbery Debate

Gnomes fought for the right to choose their own garden placement, challenging the long-standing tradition of being arbitrarily positioned among azaleas and rose bushes.

1965: The Hat Color Equality Act

A pivotal moment when gnomes of all hat colors - red, blue, green - were declared equal, ending centuries of chromatic discrimination.

1995: Official Recognition of Gnome Personhood

After decades of struggle, gnomes were legally recognized as sentient beings with rights beyond being garden accessories.
